About Me

I went to college to study art, photography, and graphic design, driven by nothing but passion and belief in a creative future. I earned my diploma, but when times grew hard and my parents could no longer afford to support my education, my formal studies came to an early end. What could have been the end of the story became the beginning of a different kind of education. Everything I know today is self-taught. Life hasn’t been gentle. I’ve worked the hardest, lowest-paying jobs — the kind that test your body and your spirit. All the while carrying a quiet, relentless dream: to become the graphic designer who finally makes it. I’ve chased opportunities, reached high, fallen short, and gotten back up more times than I can count. Sometimes the gap between where I am and where I want to be feels impossibly wide. But art has never left me. Through every setback, every exhausting shift, every moment of doubt, creativity has called my name. Design isn’t just something I do — it’s something that lives in me. It’s the constant thread running through every chapter of my life. Even when imposter syndrome whispers that I’m not good enough, not ready enough, not talented enough, my love for creating refuses to be silenced. I’ve been playing guitar since I was 15 — music, like design, is another language I speak when words fall short. I feel most at peace near the ocean; I can’t imagine life without the beach, the salt air, the horizon reminding me that there’s always something bigger ahead. I have a deep love for animals, especially dogs — their loyalty, joy, and uncomplicated love feel like anchors in a chaotic world. I am still striving. Still learning. Still building. I may not have followed the traditional path, but my journey has shaped resilience, grit, and a creative hunger that refuses to fade. I am not done. I am becoming.
